## Background: What v0.1 Got Right and Wrong

DeepFang v0.1.0 was generated by DeepSeek V4 Pro. The overall architecture — a three-stage sanitize → adjudicate → dispatch pipeline with Docker network isolation — is well-designed and genuinely useful. The problem was that two of the three pipeline containers were assigned to real open-source projects that don't expose the APIs they were expected to provide.

### The Fiction

| v0.1 Container | Image Used | What It Actually Is |
|----------------|-----------|---------------------|
| ZeroClaw sanitizer | `ghcr.io/zeroclaw-labs/zeroclaw:v0.7.4` | Personal AI assistant gateway (Rust, ~17k stars). Tag `v0.7.4` doesn't exist (current: `v0.3.3-beta`). No `/sanitize` endpoint. |
| Moltbot worker | `ghcr.io/openclaw/openclaw:latest` | "Moltbot" = "OpenClaw" = "ClawdBot" — same project, a Node.js personal assistant. Renamed Jan 2026 due to Anthropic trademark dispute. No `/execute` endpoint. |

The DeepSeek bridge (`containers/deepseek_bridge.py`) was real, complete, and correct from v0.1. The supervisor, dashboard, and docker-compose network topology were all sound.
